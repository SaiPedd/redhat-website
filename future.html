Future Interns

Advice and resources for future high school interns at Red Hat...

Week 1: Career Development

  • Orientation & Networking: Engage actively in orientation and networking events. Build relationships and seek guidance from mentors and peers.
  • Personal Brand: Create your professional brand and pitch and use these when introducing yourself in a professional setting.
  • Feedback & Reflection: Participate in feedback sessions and be open to learning. Reflect on your experiences to improve continuously.

Week 2: Tech Week

  • Job Shadowing: Observe different roles through job shadowing. Take notes and ask questions to understand various tech career paths.
  • Hands-On Learning: Dive into Learning Labs on web development, Git, UX, and pattern recognition. Apply what you learn through practical exercises.
  • Networking Opportunities: Take part in networking events and engage with others to build professional connections.
  • Balance & Reflect: Manage your time effectively between learning activities and reflection. Use journaling to consolidate your knowledge.

Week 3: All About Data

  • Build Your Own Projects: Take the knowledge you gain from data analytics and AI sessions and apply it to your own small projects. This hands-on experience will reinforce your learning.
  • Create a GitHub Account: Start a GitHub account to showcase your projects and collaborate with others. This will also help you learn version control, a crucial skill in tech.
  • Network with Data Professionals: Use the info sessions as an opportunity to connect with professionals in the data field. Prepare questions in advance and follow up with them for deeper insights.
  • Reflect and Apply: After each session, take time to reflect on what you’ve learned and consider how it applies to your interests and career goals. Document your reflections in a journal.

Week 4: Red Hat Culture

  • Immerse Yourself in the Culture: Take the time to understand and appreciate Red Hat’s unique open culture. Engage in conversations with various team members to learn more about their experiences.
  • Network Strategically: Schedule meetings with professionals whose roles interest you. Use these opportunities to gain insights and build meaningful connections.
  • Balance Work and Learning: Manage your time effectively between your projects, meetings, and personal learning. Prioritize tasks to ensure you make the most of your internship experience.
  • Seek Continuous Feedback: Regularly ask for feedback on your work and use it to improve. Be open to constructive criticism and view it as a growth opportunity.

General Tips

  • Be Proactive: Take initiative in all activities and seek out opportunities to learn and connect.
  • Stay Organized: Keep track of schedules, tasks, and deadlines.
  • Embrace Feedback: Use feedback to refine your skills and approach.
  • Ask Questions: Always ask questions, even if you feel you don’t need to. This engages the speaker and enhances your understanding.
  • Research Speakers: Research the speakers before meeting them so you have relevant topics to discuss and can make the most of your interactions.

Resources

  • Job Shadow Schedule: Access your job shadow assignments and meeting locations.
  • Program Resources: Utilize the provided links for additional information and support throughout your internship.
